Bestway Innovation Lab


In 2007 the Bestway marketing department requested innovative product designs they could use to generate new business. Being a one-person design department with a limited budget, I approached this request as a design challenge and created The Bestway International Design Internship and Innovation Lab.

 

Each year I sourced a dream team of design talent from leading institutions including Stanford, Otis College of Art and Design, The University of Illinois in Chicago and Carnegie Mellon. ABC Artboard Play Pool

Background

Initiated by the success of the interactive push button sprinkler pool, the Bestway Sales team requested further play pool concepts that explored older groups in hopes of expanding the interactive play pool range.

Users

Similar to toddlers, four to five year-olds have the same level of curiosity but their fine motor skills allow them to initiate experiences, while mentally they are able to delay gratification. Memory skills have developed and kid this age are able to identify patterns such as boy/girl gender roles, colors, words and icons. Children this age like to imitate adult activities (cooking, gardening, driving, teaching), therefore play activities that permit pretend but real-life experiences were identified as enjoyable play patterns.

Opportunities

While researching childhood development, we discovered Vygotsky's information exchange theory The Zone of Proximal Development and focused on iconic early learning activities: 123s, ABCs and arts & crafts. During Q&A with parents, we inquired about key purchases and the importance of educational toys. Not surprisingly we found parents spend more on educational toys and actively seek out art & craft experiences for their kids.
